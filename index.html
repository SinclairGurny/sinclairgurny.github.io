I'm Sinclair. I am currently working as a Machine Learning Engineer and Infrastructure Engineer at Stonewall Defense. During my 5 year coterminal program at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute I completed my M.S. in Computer Science in May 2020 and my B.S. in Computer Science and Cognitive Science in May 2019. My thesis Learning Models from Avionics Data Streams focuses on using declarative machine learning to create a data-driven system which can estimate aircraft weight in real-time during take-off. It is the only system which uses no external sensors and does real time estimation before leaving the runway making it capable of preventing accidents such as overrunning the runway. My paper based on my thesis work was accepted into the Digital Avionics Systems Conference (DASC) 2020, titled Aircraft Weight Estimation During Take-off Using Declarative Machine Learning. Computer Science 2019-2020 My graduate research in the Worldwide Computing Lab was focused on distributed data-driven systems. My thesis Learning Models from Avionics Data Streams is the only data-driven program which is designed to prevent aviation accidents due to overweight or underweight take-offs. For example Tuninter 1153 (12.5% under weight, lack of fuel), Cessna 172R N420P (6% over max weight), and Aerosucre Flight 157 (1.3% over max weight, overran runway). In more technical detail, it is focused on the applications of declarative machine-learning to estimate fixed-wing aircraft weight during take-off.
